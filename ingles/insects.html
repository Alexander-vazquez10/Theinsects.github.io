Insects, this diverse and numerous group of animals that inhabit our planet, play a fundamental role in ecosystems and in our daily lives. The classification of insects is based on their anatomy and distinctive features. These arthropods are grouped in the Insecta class, which in turn is divided into several orders, such as Coleoptera (beetles), Lepidoptera (butterflies and moths), Hymenoptera (bees and wasps), Diptera (flies and mosquitoes), among others. Each order has specific characteristics that define its behavior, its life cycle, and its role in the ecosystem.

First of all, they play a crucial role in the pollination of plants. By feeding on nectar and pollen, insects inadvertently carry pollen grains from one flower to another, allowing reproduction and the formation of fruits and seeds. Without insect pollination, many plant species could not reproduce, which would have a devastating impact on the diversity of ecosystems and food production.

In addition to pollination, insects act as natural decomposers. On the other hand, insects are an important source of food for other organisms. Many birds, reptiles, amphibians, and mammals feed on insects, which provide them with the nutrients they need for survival. However, the importance of insects goes beyond their role in ecosystems. Insects play a key role in agriculture, as some of them act as natural predators of pests that damage crops, reducing the need for chemical pesticides


Beneficial Insects

Pest control is necessary to keep the field, orchard and garden in perfect and fertile condition, so we are going to present some of the insects that will help make this possible.

Praying mantis

There is no insect that equals the praying mantis devouring insects, so you have to pay close attention to it because just as it kills insects that harm our habitat such as flies, ants or moths, they also feed on beneficial insects. Although there are insects that can control an insect infestation, it is always advisable to consult with a team of experts. If the pest has proliferated extensively, it will be necessary to carry out other types of applications.

Ladybug

One of the most well-known and loved insects by all humans are ladybugs. They live practically anywhere and believe it or not, they are great predators of aphids, so much so that they only lay their eggs if they know for sure that they are in the presence of aphids. In addition to aphids, these are predators of insects such as whiteflies, mites, spiders... And you can find them in plants with yellow flowers such as sunflowers or chrysanthemums.

Wasps

Wasps can become double-edged insects, they are ideal for controlling other insect pests such as whitefly or bedbugs since wasp hatchlings feed only on insect larvae. But also, if we do not control them, they can be a harmful pest for humans.

Bees

These small companions feed on the nectar of some flowers and pollinate the plants around them, carrying said pollen. So these are very necessary for the formation of flowers and fruits of all crops.
